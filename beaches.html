Philippine Beaches

in Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao Islands.

Luzon

Coron

Best wrecking diving destination in the country. Apart from wrecking diving, Coron's also known for it's famous karst limestone cliffs, turquoise water and all around breathtaking scenery.

Things To Do

El Nido

El Nido recently ranked number four on Code Nast Travelers "20 Most Beautiful Beaches in the World" list. See limestone cliffs, Turquoise water and laying on white sand Beaches.

Things To Do

Puerto Princesa

Puerto Princesa is a coastal city on Palawan Island in the western Philippines. It's a base for boat trips through the massive limestone caves and underground river of the biodiverse Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park.

Things To Do

Puerto Galera

Puerto Galera is a town on the Philippine Island of Mindoro. It's known for dive sites and beaches. The long curve of White Beach is hacked by bars and Resorts. Inland, a forest trail leads to secluded Talipanan Falls.

Things To Do

Visayas

Boracay Island

Boracay, one of the top Islands in the Philippines, is popular for its beaches and blessed with a long stretch of powdery white sand, crystal clear and azure waters, a stunning sunset and bustling nightlife.

Things To Do

Panglao Island

Panglao Island, One of the fastest-growing tourist destinations in the country, Panglao Island in Bohol is a dream gateway especially for couples who are looking for that much-needed reprieve from the hustle and bustles of their every life.

Things To Do

Lambug Island

Lambug Beach, located in the Philippines, is renowned for its breathtaking natural allure, featuring pristine powdery white sands and crystal-clear azure waters.

Things To Do

Mindanao

Dakak

The famous Dakak Beach is most known for its strikingly shaped bay and powdery white sand, and as a diving Mecca in Mindanao. Perfect blue waters, tremendous dive sites with great quantity of coral reefs, and a wonderful sunset scope characterize this tropical Eden.

Things To Do

Camiguin Island

Camiguin, or also known as the “The Island Born of Fire”, is a pear-shaped volcanic island that nested in Northern Mindanao particularly seated in the Bohol Sea. The province is famous for its beautiful landscape, water attractions, historical, and cultural background.

Things To Do

Siargao Island

Siargao is a tear-drop shaped island in the Philippine Sea situated 196 kilometers southeast of Tacloban. It has a land area of approximately 437 square kilometres. The east coast is relatively straight with one deep inlet, Port Pilar.
